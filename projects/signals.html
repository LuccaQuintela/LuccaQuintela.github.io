Overview

In the context of particle decay physics reactions, the act of trying to find the specific reaction you want can often be very complicated. There are millions of reactions every second and they last for picoseconds.

So my partner and I built various different machine learning models to classify instances of a certain decay reaction to help physicists explore the boundaries of the current theoretical physics models(a B meson particle decaying into a Kaon and 2 Leptons). Our goal was to build several different models and to then compare their results in the end.

I built multiple different neural network architectures, with distinct patterns in the size, shape and count of their layers, while my partner built a boosted decision tree. We then both iterated on our models to fine-tune the optimal hyperparameters.
